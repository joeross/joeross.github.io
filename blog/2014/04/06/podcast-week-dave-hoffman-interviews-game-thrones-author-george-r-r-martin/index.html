Podcast of the Week: Dave Hoffman interviews Game of Thrones author George R.R. Martin

I took corporations law class with Professor Dave Hoffman at Temple Law. I’ve followed his work and his blogging ever since I found out he is a rather prolific blogger himself.1 In 2007, he interviewed George R.R. Martin, author of the A Song of Ice and Fire series on which HBO’s hit television show Game of Thrones is based. The two discussed fan fiction, inheritance law and the lack of lawyers in Martin’s work.

Professor Hoffman provides a transcription of the entire interview at the popular law blog he co-founded, Concurring Opinions. Read part 1 here, part 2 here and part 3 here. Whether you prefer the book series or HBO’s Game of Thrones, it’s worth a listen.

Caution: I didn’t screen very well for spoilers because I’m on book four already, so if you haven’t read that far or seen Game of Thrones through season three, you may want to listen with one finger on the PAUSE button.

I came to the A Song of Ice and Fire books late, only finding the series after watching the first few episodes of Game of Thrones. But I’ve stayed one book ahead of the television show ever since. As the fourth season premiers tonight, I thought it appropriate to share this excellent interview by Professor Hoffman. You can find a link to the audio file at the original Concurring Opinions post about the interview. Enjoy.
