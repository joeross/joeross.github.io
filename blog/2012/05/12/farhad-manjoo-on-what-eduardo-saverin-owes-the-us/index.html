Farhad Manjoo on What Eduardo Saverin Owes the US

May 12th, 2012 | Comments

Farhad Manjoo, writing at PandoDaily:

> > At just about every step between his landing in Miami and his becoming a co-founder of Facebook, you find American institutions and inventions playing a significant part in his success. > >

Manjoo doesn’t agree with Saverin’s decision to renounce US citizenship just in time to make sure his expected Facebook-IPO windfall escapes capital gains tax, netting him an extra ~$600 million. Manjoo, like Saverin, found a life in the United States he could never have had in his home country.

For what it’s worth, it may not be such a clear-cut windfall, and Saverin is certainly not alone.

Regardless, I understand Manjoo’s position, but unfortunately it’s not realistic. Like I said in a comment on the article, American individuals and businesses avoid as much taxation as lawfully possible every year. Unless Saverin is breaking the law, the rest is probably clickbait.
