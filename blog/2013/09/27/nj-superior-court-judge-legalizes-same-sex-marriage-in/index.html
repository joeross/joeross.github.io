NJ Superior Court judge legalizes same-sex marriage in the state

Sep 27th, 2013 | Comments

NJ Superior Court judge legalizes same-sex marriage in the state

> > Superior Court Judge Mary Jacobson granted an emergency request by six gay couples, ordering state officials to begin officiating same-sex marriages on Oct. 21. > >

This is really great news.

But…

As is so often the case in litigation, this is by no means the end of the issue in New Jersey. From the Superior Court, the Christie administration’s lawyers can appeal up to the state Supreme Court if they need to.

One of their arguments, and the one that is sure to be front-and-center in any appellate brief they file, is that the Superior Court lacked subject-matter jurisdiction because the marriage benefits in question were federal, not state, benefits.

It’s worth noting those who get married under today’s decision may eventually find themselves in a legal gray area if a higher court reverses the outcome.
